11.02.2011

Eddie Grey’s Debut Album Release Party


“THE LOVE THIEF”

           LOS ANGELES, November 1st 2011 – Eddie Grey’s highly anticipated debut album, The Love Thief, is here! The record release party takes place on Saturday, November 19th, 2011, at The Hotel Café (1623 N. Cahuenga Blvd.). Eddie Grey consists of singer/songwriter/guitarist, Eddie Grey, drummer/percussionist, Patrick Coggins, and bass player, Kevin Comden. Juan “Swift” Zavala and TJ Rivera produced the album. Eddie Grey, alongside his official sponsors, BAND FOR A CURE, inKLUDE Me!, PaxStereo TV, and Kraven Magazine invite you to attend an exclusive party to celebrate the release of his much talked about album.
           The Love Thief acquired its’ name from the idea that Eddie has been influenced by, or has ‘stolen’ from many musical genres in the creation of this compilation of songs. There are no boundaries when it comes to love and there are no boundaries when it comes to great music. Eddie has taken the best parts of all styles of music and has brought them together in a way that will speak to audiences across the ages and different cultural backgrounds.
           Members of the Eddie Grey project have an incredible history with music, which includes playing along side the Plain White T’s, subbing in for Alien Ant Farm drummer, Mike Cosgrove alongside Hyro Da Hero’s Texas tour, and collaborating on songs with Baby Bash, Mana, Johnny Gill, Paula D’Anda, Eddie Murphy, and Nicholas “RAS” Furlong.
            Growing up in South Gate and Compton, California, Eddie had a rather chaotic childhood, in which he found his comfort by being immersed in music. He escaped the hectic lifestyle of his youth by studying any type of music he could get his hands on and played in bands of many genres, including rock, punk, and jazz. By having a genuine appreciation for music from all walks of life, Eddie has been inspired and was destined to make this eclectic album for the public to enjoy. Eddie’s upbringing is the reason why he feels the need to give back to the community, since music paved the way for a good life. A portion of the proceeds of the night will go directly towards BAND FOR A CURE (http://bandforacure.org), a non-profit that supports cancer research and treatment through music.
            The Hotel Café stage has been graced by artists such as John Mayer, Jason Mraz, Neil Young, Crosby, Stills and Nash, Joni Mitchell, and now it’s time for unsigned musician, Eddie Grey to be added to the venue performance highlights. Everyone is guaranteed to find a track that speaks directly to them when Eddie performs his favorite songs from The Love Thief, live throughout the night. Songs on the album include those with a reggae feel, some hard rock, a few funky beats, as well as mainstream pop sounds.
Eddie Grey’s consistent interaction with the audience during his high-energy shows keeps the crowd laughing and the room alive.
